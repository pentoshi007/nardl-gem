# Final Paper Blueprint
## Publication-Safe Integrated Design for a 7,000–8,000 Word Journal-Style Paper

**Project context:** This document is the master handoff note for writing the final paper from the current project. It is designed so that any future AI or human writer can convert it into a complete LaTeX manuscript, PDF, and DOCX version **without changing the empirical story**, **without overclaiming the evidence**, **without exceeding the strict length budget**, and **without losing formatting quality across output formats**.

**Hard paper constraint:** The final output must be a **journal-style paper**, not a full dissertation chapter manuscript.

- **Target length:** 7,000–8,000 words total, including main text, footnotes, and references where possible
- **Target page length:** 15–20 pages maximum in normal journal formatting, allowing important figures to appear in the body where they are discussed
- **Preferred structure:** 6–8 major sections
- **Main text discipline:** only the strongest results should appear in the main paper
- **Appendix discipline:** all overflow detail must go to appendix or be removed

**Core principle:** The final paper should **not** be framed as a generic “WPI asymmetry paper” and should **not** be framed as a strong “headline CPI asymmetry paper.” The safest and most publishable design is an **integrated transmission-and-attenuation paper** that combines:
- long-horizon wholesale inflation evidence,
- retail fuel transmission evidence,
- fuel-sensitive consumer-price evidence,
- and the attenuation of oil shocks before they reach headline CPI.

This integrated architecture provides the paper’s **novel contribution**.

# 2. Instructions for any future AI or human writer

If you are using this file to generate LaTex:

- Write a **complete journal-style paper manuscript** in academic style.
- Use a **formal economics paper structure**.
- Do **not invent results**, coefficients, p-values, diagnostics, references, dates, or tables.

- Keep the paper **publication-safe**, meaning:
  - no exaggerated novelty claims,
  - no unsupported “first ever” language,
  - no claiming structural causality where the evidence is only predictive or associational,
  - no presenting appendix results as main-text proof.
- Keep the paper **within a hard size budget**:
  - 7,000–8,000 words total
  - 15–20 pages maximum
  - no more than 5 main tables
  - important figures should appear inline near their first substantive discussion, not grouped at the end
  - no more than 4 main figures

  - it must convert cleanly to PDF and DOCX
  - figure placement, caption hierarchy, and section order should survive DOCX export

- Use **restrained empirical language** such as:
  - “broadly consistent with”

  - “materially larger”

  - “proves”

# 3. Final recommended paper identity

## 3.1 Best final design

The final paper should be built around the following integrated question:

> How do global oil shocks transmit through India’s pricing system across wholesale prices, retail fuel, fuel-sensitive consumer prices, and headline inflation, and where does pass-through weaken?

This is better than either of the following weaker framings:
- “Does oil have asymmetric pass-through to headline CPI?”
- “Does oil affect WPI in India?”

The integrated framing is stronger because it:
- preserves the long historical WPI contribution,
- uses the CPI work instead of discarding it,
- creates a clearer mechanism story,
- and adds publication-relevant novelty through cross-layer transmission mapping.

---

## 3.2 Best one-sentence contribution

Use a contribution statement like this:

> This dissertation contributes to the India oil pass-through literature by integrating long-horizon wholesale inflation evidence with retail fuel and consumer-price transmission layers, showing that oil shocks transmit strongly through wholesale and fuel-sensitive prices but attenuate sharply before reaching headline CPI.

Alternative version:

> Rather than testing a single inflation measure in isolation, this study traces oil-price transmission across wholesale, retail fuel, sectoral consumer, and headline consumer layers of India’s price system, highlighting both reform-era strengthening and attenuation toward household inflation.

---

## 3.3 What the novelty is

The novelty claim should be **modest but real**.

### Allowed novelty language
- “The paper integrates multiple price layers in one empirical design.”
- “The paper jointly studies wholesale, retail fuel, fuel-sensitive CPI, and headline CPI transmission.”
- “The paper reframes Indian oil pass-through as a layered transmission-and-attenuation process.”
- “The paper combines long-horizon WPI evidence with fuel-channel and consumer-price evidence in a single study.”
- “The paper offers a unified empirical map of where imported oil inflation is amplified and where it is absorbed.”

### Not allowed novelty language
- “This is the first paper ever to study oil and inflation in India.”
- “This is the first WPI pass-through paper.”
- “This is the definitive paper on Indian oil inflation.”
- “This paper proves a completely new theory.”

### Best novelty formulation
Use something like:

> The paper’s novelty lies not in proposing a new estimator, but in integrating a long historical wholesale-inflation series with fuel-channel and consumer-inflation layers to identify where imported oil shocks are transmitted and where they are diluted in the Indian pricing system.

That is accurate and publication-safe.

# 5. Final research questions

## 5.1 Main research question
> How do global oil shocks transmit through India’s pricing system, and at which stages is pass-through strongest or weakest?

## 5.2 Secondary research questions
1. Is oil-price pass-through stronger in wholesale prices than in headline consumer prices?
2. Does the fuel channel provide the main mechanism linking crude oil to domestic inflation?
3. Did pass-through become stronger after the post-2010 reform and deregulation period?
4. Is short-run asymmetry a central feature of the data, or is the more robust pattern one of layered transmission with attenuation?
5. Does nonlinear long-run evidence supplement, rather than dominate, the short-run ADL findings?

# 6. Final hypotheses

Use a restrained hypothesis structure.

## H1: Wholesale pass-through hypothesis
Oil-price shocks pass through significantly to India’s headline WPI over the long historical sample.

## H2: Fuel-channel amplification hypothesis
Pass-through is materially stronger in fuel-sensitive price layers than in headline inflation layers.

## H3: Attenuation hypothesis
The magnitude of pass-through declines as oil shocks move from crude to retail fuel to fuel-sensitive consumer prices and then to headline CPI.

## H4: Reform-era strengthening hypothesis
Pass-through is materially larger in the post-2010 period than in the pre-2010 period.

## H5: Short-run symmetry hypothesis
In short-run ADL specifications, the data do not provide strong evidence of asymmetric pass-through.

## H6: Supplementary nonlinear long-run hypothesis
Nonlinear levels models may detect long-run asymmetry, but these results should be treated as supplementary rather than as the sole basis of the paper’s contribution.

# 9. Best final model hierarchy for a short paper

## 9.1 Main claim-bearing models

The main paper should use only the minimum model set needed to tell one coherent story.

### Model A — Historical anchor
**Headline WPI ADL using INR-denominated oil**
- This is the main historical result.
- It supports the claim that oil shocks pass into wholesale inflation over a long horizon.
- This should be the central claim-bearing equation in the paper.

### Model B — Retail fuel mechanism
**PPAC petrol model**
- This is the cleanest first-stage domestic transmission channel.
- It is essential to the integrated novelty of the paper.

### Model C — Headline CPI attenuation endpoint
**Headline CPI asymmetric ADL**
- This should **not** be used to claim strong headline CPI asymmetry.
- It should be used to show that pass-through weakens sharply by the time the shock reaches headline CPI.
- This is essential to the attenuation story.

### Model D — One fuel-sensitive supporting layer
Use **either**:
- **WPI Fuel & Power ADL**, if the paper emphasizes the 30-year fuel-sensitive wholesale layer,
- **or** **PPAC -> CPI Fuel & Light**, if the paper emphasizes the consumer bridge more strongly.

Default preference for the short paper:
- use **WPI Fuel & Power ADL** in the main text,
- move **CPI Fuel & Light bridge** to appendix or one short discussion paragraph.

## 9.3 Appendix-only models

Use NARDL only as supplementary evidence:

- the integrated paper’s novelty is the layered transmission architecture, not nonlinear estimation alone,

# 10. Key empirical findings that the paper may use

These are the anchor findings the final dissertation can report.

- `CPT- = 0.0374`, `p = 0.0012`
- asymmetry `p = 0.6727`
- bootstrap `p = 0.7461`

Interpretation:
- wholesale inflation responds significantly to oil shocks,
- short-run asymmetry is not supported.

## 10.2 WPI Fuel & Power ADL
- sample span: about 30.92 years
- `CPT+ = 0.2866`, `p < 0.001`
- `CPT- = 0.2677`, `p < 0.001`
- asymmetry `p = 0.7832`

Interpretation:
- the fuel-sensitive wholesale layer is much more responsive than headline WPI.

## 10.3 Headline WPI pre/post-2010
Pre-2010:
- `CPT+ = 0.0117`, `p = 0.3812`
- `CPT- = 0.0253`, `p = 0.0430`

Post-2010:
- `CPT+ = 0.0741`, `p = 0.0043`
- `CPT- = 0.0813`, `p < 0.001`

Interpretation:
- pass-through is materially stronger in the later regime.

## 10.4 PPAC retail fuel model
- `CPT+ = 0.3459`, `p < 0.001`
- `CPT- = 0.1912`, `p < 0.001`
- asymmetry around 10% significance margin

Interpretation:
- retail fuel is the strongest domestic transmission layer.

## 10.5 CPI Fuel & Light bridge
- PPAC -> CPI Fuel & Light `CPT+ = 0.1777`, `p = 0.0021`
- asymmetry not supported

Interpretation:
- consumer fuel prices absorb a meaningful part of retail fuel pass-through.

## 10.6 Headline CPI endpoint
- `CPT+ = 0.0213`, `p = 0.1220`
- `CPT- = 0.0006`, `p = 0.9375`
- asymmetry `p = 0.2408`
- bootstrap `p = 0.4997`

Interpretation:
- the direct effect on headline CPI is weak and statistically modest,
- this supports attenuation rather than strong headline CPI asymmetry.

# 19. Recommended ordering of results

The order of empirical presentation matters a lot.

## Best order
1. Historical headline WPI result
2. Retail fuel mechanism result
3. One fuel-sensitive layer result
4. Headline CPI endpoint
5. Integrated attenuation comparison
6. Pre/post-2010 interpretation
7. Very short supplementary note on appendix nonlinear evidence

## Why this order works
It tells a publication-safe story within a short paper:
- start where evidence is strongest,
- show the transmission mechanism early,
- show where transmission weakens,
- then end with compact reform and supplementary interpretation.

Do **not** start with CPI asymmetry.
Do **not** start with NARDL.
Do **not** let the fuel bridge crowd out the main narrative.

# 21. Suggested dissertation abstract blueprint

Use something close to the following when writing the final abstract:

> This dissertation examines how global oil shocks transmit through India’s pricing system across wholesale prices, retail fuel, fuel-sensitive consumer prices, and headline inflation. Rather than treating inflation as a single index, the study develops an integrated transmission-and-attenuation framework that combines a long historical wholesale price series with retail fuel and consumer-price layers. Using monthly time-series methods with robust inference, the analysis shows that oil-price pass-through is strongest in retail fuel and fuel-sensitive wholesale prices, remains statistically significant in headline WPI, and attenuates sharply before reaching headline CPI. Subsample evidence indicates materially stronger pass-through in the post-2010 period, consistent with a more market-linked pricing regime. Short-run asymmetry is not a robust central finding in the preferred ADL specifications, while nonlinear levels models provide supplementary evidence of long-run asymmetry. The results suggest that imported oil inflation in India is best understood as a layered process in which domestic fuel pricing and sectoral composition determine how much of the external shock eventually reaches household inflation.

This abstract can be polished later, but its structure should remain.

# 24. What to say about WPI vs CPI

Use the following logic:

- WPI should not be presented as “better” in a universal welfare sense.
- CPI should not be presented as the only valid inflation measure.
- Instead, explain that they answer different questions.

### Best wording
> WPI is more informative about upstream cost transmission and imported input-price pressures, whereas CPI is more informative about household-facing inflation. Studying both jointly allows the dissertation to distinguish where oil shocks enter the price system from whether they survive all the way to headline consumer inflation.

This is an excellent publication-safe line.

# 25. What to say about asymmetry

Asymmetry must be handled carefully.

## Main text position
> Short-run asymmetry is not the dissertation’s central result.

## Better framing
> The main contribution is the mapping of layered transmission and attenuation. Short-run asymmetry is tested, but it is not strongly supported in the preferred ADL specifications.

## Appendix position
> Nonlinear long-run models provide supplementary evidence that asymmetry may appear in equilibrium adjustment, but these results are secondary to the main short-run transmission evidence.

This avoids the trap of overselling weak asymmetry results.

# 26. What to say about Granger results

Never call them structural causality.

Use:
- “Granger-predictive evidence”
- “predictive precedence”
- “lead-lag evidence”

Not:
- “causal proof”
- “causal direction established”

A safe sentence:
> The Granger results are interpreted as predictive evidence that oil-price movements precede changes in wholesale and fuel-sensitive price layers, not as definitive structural causality.
